Circular Economy Business Model Generator - Steps Guide

Step 1: Define the circular initiative (CI)
Use the CI Description sheet. Example: To offer the product P through a product-service system, which includes product leasing, installation, repair and maintenance, and removal at the end of use for recycling back into same product P.
Step 2: Select business process strategies needed to implement the CI
Use the Business Processes sheet.
Step 3: Select the circularity strategies needed to implement the CI
Use the Circularity Assessment sheet.
Step 4: State the key objectives
Use the Key Objectives sheet. Define combinations of business processes and CE strategies to select KPIs.
Step 5: Select the appropriate KPIs
Use the KPI Database (5.1) to filter and select indicators. Selected KPIs appear in Selected KPIs (5.2).
Step 6: Compare the Circular Economy Initiative scenarios
Use the Compare CI Scenarios sheet to calculate and compare indicator values across scenarios.
Step 7: Assess the current business model
Use the Business Model Assessment questionnaire to identify problems, trends, strategies, and enablers.
Step 8: Check alignment with business model patterns
The Business Model Patterns sheet shows 24 patterns with alignment scores based on your assessment.
Step 9: Select potential design options
Use Design Options Database (9.1) to select options for up to 3 business model configurations. Selected options appear in (9.2).
Step 10: Calculate investment returns
Use Cash Flow Calculation (10.1) for revenues and costs over 20 years. Economic Indicators (10.2) shows IRR, NPV, and Payback period.
